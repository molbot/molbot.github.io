Spaghetti Squash

You may be wondering to yourself right now about why I chose to make a page dedicated to Spaghetti Squash, this is not just a cool new food fad, its a fun, healthy , and protein packed meal that more people should be aware of... if you ask me.

In case you have never encountered a spaghetti squash, they are large oval squashes that have a melon-like appearence. The color of their rinds can range from crayola YELLOW It is referred to as Spahghetti squash becuase when cooked, the inside becomes string like... resembling angel hair pasta.

When preparing the Spaghetti Squash, one should treat it the same way as if you were about to carve a pumpkin for halloween. Meaning the seeds on the inside need to be scooped out so you can get to the good stuff! Once thw insides have been scooped out, the way you choose to cook your squash is completely up to you. My personal favorite is to bake it in the oven and cook additional topping separately. If baking is not your favorite way to cook, you can also steam, roast, and even microwave your Spaghetti Squash.

Ingredients

  • 1 Spaghetti Squash
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1.5 cups of chopped tomatoes
  • crumbled feta cheese
  • chopped mushrooms
  • anything else you heart desires

Directions

  • Preheat the oven to a cool 350 degrees F.
  • While the oven is preheating, cut the spaghetti squash in half, and scoop out the insides.
  • After the insides have been removed, lightly cover both halves with the vegetable oil.
  • Place the squash on a cooking sheet and place in the oven once preheated.
  • Cook add-ins to your desired liking while the squash is cooking for 30 minutes.
  • Once all parts are cooked, add everything your heart could ever want into the Spaghetti Squash!
